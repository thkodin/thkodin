### Publication(s)

- [Low Cost 3D Motion Capture of Rapid Maneuvers Using a Single High Speed Camera](https://doi.org/10.1002/cav.70070)
  <details>
  <summary>My Role</summary>

  I am a core developer/contributor of [MARTy](https://github.com/Asad127/Lights-Camera-Mirrors-Action-Toolbox-for-3D-Analysis-of-High-rate-Maneuvers-Using-a-Single-Camer), having written the vast majority of the source code, documentation, tutorials, and compiling several results reported in the paper. I also proposed solutions to some of the key problems we observed with the mirror acting as a substitute camera, e.g., highlighting the need for the permutation matrix, etc.

  Originally, I was onboarded onto this project within the Robotics and Machine Intelligence (ROMI) Lab at SEECS, NUST by my thesis supervisor W. Hussain around the mid of 2022. I worked closely with both my supervisor and A. Khan. Most of the development was wrapped up by mid 2023, with some additional work done mid 2025 to address some feedback which ultimately led to publication.        
  </details>

<details>
<summary><h3>A Summary of My Journey</h3></summary>

I am (was?) an electrical engineering undergrad who realized a little too late that he did not like electrical engineering.

I did, however, _really_ like the programming courses part of that program, and my FYP leaned more heavily into the Computer Science department: a (pretty basic) realtime sentry-like targeting system built on a Raspberry Pi using image processing.

So naturally, the next step was a ~~Bachelors in Compute Science~~ Masters in Artificial Intelligence & Autonomous Systems from [NUST](https://nust.edu.pk/). Here, I had the opportunity to develop several projects in Python for the courses I studied, and my thesis was to design a text-based CAPTCHA using [knot topologies in 3D space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knot_theory). While I did complete the thesis, I am much more focused on my professional career at the moment so publishing this is currently on my bucket list (much to the dismay of my supervisor).

In parallel with my Masters, I freelanced for a while, until a friend referred me to the amazing folks at Antematter early 2024, where I interviewed, got the job, and am currently working in the capacity of an AI Engineer and generally as a Software Engineer.

Through Antematter, I also worked at [3ECo](https://www.3eco.com/) as a talent partner for ~1.75 years (Jun 2024 &ndash; Mar 2026), where my initial focus was on building AI applications and workflows for internal teams. After about a year though, this focus shifted to AI Enablement, Discoverability, and Governance for agentic communication and workflows both internally within the company and externally to clients using the open source [Agent-to-Agent Protocol](https://a2a-protocol.org/latest/specification/).

Nowadays, I am working on an internal tool at Antematter inspired by the concept of agent factories.
</details>
